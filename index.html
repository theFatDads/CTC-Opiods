The Next Step.

When using or abusing opioids, you need to focus on taking steps towards further recovery. TheNextStep is designed to help you achieve those steps.

Step 1: Understand the Impacts and Seek Alternatives

Opioids are a group of natural or synthetic drugs used by individuals for pain relief When used as prescribed, opioids are extremely effective. However, even when they are taken as prescribed, there is extreme potential for abuse and addiction.

Repeated use can lead to physical dependence in just a matter of weeks, and psychological dependence in only a matter of days. Therefore, The Next Step encourages those in need of painkillers to search for other options. Over the counter alternatives as well as non-drug treatments that can be used for pain control that are readily available. These alternatives are much safer and more accessible. Prescribed opioids can be used in dire situations, but under extreme caution.

Step 3: Dispose of leftover.

If you are done using your prescription, you should dispose of these drugs immediately. Proper disposal is crucial. These drugs should be dropped off at one of many different disposal locations. By not disposing of them, you put both yourself and family at risk. This reduces the chance that you or your loved ones accidentally take the drug or intentionally misuse the drug.
